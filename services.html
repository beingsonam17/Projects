Cockroach

Cockroaches, though unsightly and disgusting, may seem harmless enough. The truth is that a roach infestation in your home can lead to serious health risks. Certain proteins found in cockroach feces, saliva, and body parts can cause allergic reactions or trigger asthma symptoms, especially in children. Cockroaches also passively transport microbes on their body surfaces, including pathogens and bacteria that are potentially dangerous to humans such as E. coli and Salmonella.

Ant

Ant populations can grow remarkably fast, so what started out as a few ants here and there can quickly turn into a very serious infestation. Although ant bites do not cause disease or serious injury, they can be painful and constant itching can lead to excessive scratching that in turn increases the chances of secondary skin infection. Some ant species, particularly the carpenter ant, can destroy wood and other building materials by excavating tunnels.

Mosquito

Each summer, hordes of these insects descend on backyards, parks, and campgrounds. While most mosquitoes are merely nuisances, some can transmit serious diseases such as encephalitis and malaria in humans and heartworm disease in dogs. Even if the mosquitoes on your property are disease-free, they can bite incessantly, drawing blood and leaving you feeling itchy and uncomfortable.
